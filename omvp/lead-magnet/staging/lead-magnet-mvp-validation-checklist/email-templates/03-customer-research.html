The Art of Customer Discovery

Most founders ask terrible questions and get useless answers. Today, you'll learn the exact interview framework that's uncovered insights for billion-dollar companies.

üéØ The Golden Rule of Customer Interviews

"Ask about their past, not your future."

Focus on what they've already done, tried, and experienced. Their past behavior predicts future behavior better than their opinions about your idea.

üìã The 15-Minute Customer Interview Script

Opening (2 minutes)

"Thanks for your time. I'm researching [problem area] and would love to learn about your experience. There are no wrong answers - I'm just trying to understand how you currently handle [situation]."

Problem Discovery (5 minutes)

  • "Tell me about the last time you experienced [problem]"
  • "How do you currently solve this?"
  • "What's frustrating about your current approach?"
  • "Have you tried other solutions? What happened?"

Solution Validation (5 minutes)

  • "If you had a magic wand, how would you solve this?"
  • "What would need to be true for you to switch to a new solution?"
  • "How much time/money does this problem cost you?"
  • "Who else is involved in solving this problem?"

Network Expansion (3 minutes)

  • "Who else do you know who has this problem?"
  • "Would you be interested in seeing what I build?"
  • "Can I follow up with my findings?"

‚úÖ DO THIS

  • Ask about specific recent examples
  • Dig into emotions and frustrations
  • Listen for words they use
  • Ask "tell me more about that"
  • Stay curious, not defensive

‚ùå DON'T DO THIS

  • Pitch your solution
  • Ask leading questions
  • Accept vague answers
  • Ask "would you use this?"
  • Talk more than you listen

üö® Red Flag Responses

If you hear these, dig deeper or find different customers:

  • "That's an interesting idea" (they're being polite)
  • "I would definitely use that" (no specifics = no commitment)
  • "I don't really have that problem" (wrong customer segment)
  • "I'll think about it" (not urgent enough for them)
