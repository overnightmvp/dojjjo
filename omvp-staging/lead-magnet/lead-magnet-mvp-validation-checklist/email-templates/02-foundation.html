The Validation Mindset That Changes Everything

Yesterday you started your setup. Today, we're going to rewire how you think about validation. This mindset shift is worth $10,000+ in avoided mistakes.

üéØ Key Insight: You're Not Validating Your Idea

You're validating your assumptions. Your idea is just a collection of assumptions about problems, solutions, and customers. Most founders try to prove their idea is right. Smart founders try to prove their assumptions are wrong.

üìã The 3-Layer Validation Framework

1
Problem Validation
Does the problem you think exists actually exist? Do people care enough to pay for a solution?
Danger: Solving problems people have but don't care about
2
Solution Validation
Is your proposed solution something people actually want? Would they choose it over alternatives?
Danger: Building the wrong solution to the right problem
3
Market Validation
Can you reach your customers? Will they pay what you need to charge? Is the market big enough?
Danger: Great product, impossible business model

‚ö†Ô∏è The #1 Validation Mistake

Asking people if they like your idea. Everyone will say yes to be polite. Instead, ask about their current behavior, pain points, and what they've tried before. Watch what they do, not what they say.

üìù Your Day 2 Assignment:

  1. List 5 core assumptions about your idea (problem size, target customer, solution fit, pricing, distribution)
  2. Rank them by risk - which assumption, if wrong, would kill your business?
  3. Identify 3 potential customers to interview (people who have the problem you're solving)
